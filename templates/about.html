What is arXiv?

arXiv represents a fundamental shift in the dissemination of scholarly work, providing a free-to-access platform that hosts a vast collection of preprints across a multitude of scientific disciplines. Established in 1991, it serves as a crucial repository for researchers worldwide, offering early versions of research papers before they undergo the formal peer review process.

The repository covers a rich tapestry of fields such as mathematics, physics, astronomy, computer science, quantitative biology, finance, and statistics. By facilitating open access, arXiv breaks down barriers to knowledge sharing and accelerates the pace of discovery. It empowers authors to present their findings promptly to the global scientific community and receive feedback that can refine their work further. For readers and fellow researchers, it serves as a real-time pulse of the latest scientific advancements.

Embraced by academics and researchers as a key resource, arXiv fosters a collaborative environment where ideas are exchanged freely, critiqued constructively, and built upon robustly. It stands as a testament to the collective drive for knowledge advancement and the democratization of information in the digital age.
